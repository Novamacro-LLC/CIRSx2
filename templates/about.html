About us

Our Guiding Principles

Welcome to CIRSx.com, your premier resource for information and solutions regarding Chronic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (CIRS) and related environmental health challenges. At CIRSx, we are dedicated to advancing the understanding, treatment, and prevention of CIRS through evidence-based research, cutting-edge technology, and comprehensive educational resources.

Scott McMahon, MD
Founder
Medical Expert

Dr. McMahon was the first physician certified in the Shoemaker Protocol for treatment of CIRS.  Trained by Dr. Shoemaker himself, he has also collaborated in research, authored or co-authored a dozen scientific papers and 3 medical textbook chapters, delivered more than 50 CIRS presentations at statewide, national and international medical meetings, taught Proficiency Partners, testified as an expert in 10 states and co-authored and co-edited a book on CIRS.

Greg Weatherman, CMC
Founder
Environmental Expert

Greg is a co-founder of CIRSx and has been in the industry since 1997 working with mold in the Washington DC area. He holds a Virginia Class A contractor license to include asbestos abatement.  He specializes in testing of mold and bacteria from water damaged indoor environments.

Eric Dorninger, ND

The CIRSx Mission

Our mission is to create and refine standards by staying on the cutting edge and using an evidence-based approach. We aim to transform multiple industries and their approaches to Chronic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (CIRS) and other environmentally related health challenges.

By working with established methods, we test and integrate new technologies to achieve better outcomes. This commitment ensures that our standards not only meet current needs but also anticipate future challenges, driving innovation and improvement in healthcare and environmental services.

02

Our Vision & Strategy

-To identify the environmental problems, identify knowledge gaps, contribute to and perform research, develop solutions and teach both the problems and solutions to the environmental and medical worlds.

-To further educate other realms such as legal, layperson, medical and residential/commercial insurance worlds.

-To create new standards and refine older standards by reviewing literature, professional experiences and collected data allowing the evidence to guide best practices.

-To create a safe place for varying disciplines, such as environmental professionals, healthcare providers and others, to interface and interact to develop multi-industry solutions for environmental problems.

-To develop a repository of trusted educational and research materials and make these resources available to the different worlds.
